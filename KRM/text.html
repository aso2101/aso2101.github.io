ಪ್ರಥಮ ಜರಿಚ್ಛೇದಂ

First Chapter

ಶ್ರೀ ತಳ್ತುರದೊಳ್ ಕೌಸ್ತುಭ
ಜಾತದ್ಯುತಿ ಬಳಸಿ ಕಾಂಡಪಟದಂತಿರೆ ಸಂ ಪ್ರೀತಿಯಿನಾವನನಗಲಳ್
ನೀತಿನಿರಂತರನುದಾರನಾ ನೃಪತುಂಗಂ (೧)
The goddess Shri stands by him,
the light borne of the Kaustubha jewel
on his chest surrounding them like a screen,
and out of her attachment to him
she does not leave.
He is Nripatunga, the exalted,
completely suffused with wisdom.1
ಕೃತಕೃತ್ಯಮಲ್ಲನಪ್ರತಿ
ಹತವಿಕ್ರಮನೊಸೆದು ವೀರನಾರಾಯಣನ ಪ್ಪತಿಶಯಧವಳಂ ನಮಗೀ
ಗತರ್ಕಿತೋಪಸ್ಥಿತ ಪ್ರತಾಪೋದಯಮಂ (೨)
The wrestler who performs his duties,
the one whose valor cannot be checked,
the one who is the Heroic Narayana himself,
the one who is miraculously white—
may he be pleased to grant us
the never-before-imagined dawn of
fiery brilliance that is at hand.2
ಶ್ರೀ ವಿಶದವರ್ಣೆ ಮಧುರಾ
ರಾವೋಚಿತೆ ಚತುರರುಚಿರ ಪದರಚನೆ ಚಿರಂ ದೇವಿ ಸರಸ್ವತಿ ಹಂಸೀ
ಭಾವದೆ ನೆಲೆಗೊಳ್ಗೆ ಕೂರ್ತು ಮನ್ಮಾನಸದೊಳ್ (೩)
With a brilliant white color
      to match her faultless speech,
with a sweet and pleasant cry
      to match her singing,
and with footsteps
      to match her skillful and resplendent compositions,
may the Goddess Sarasvati be pleased
to become a Royal Goose and stay for a long time
in the Manasa Lake of my mind.3
ಪರಮಾಲಂಕಾರೋಚಿತ
ವಿರಚನೆಗಳ್ ನೆಗೞ್ಗುಮಾರ ವದನೋದರದೊಳ್ ನೆರಮಕ್ಕಾ ಪರಮ ಕವೀ
ಶ್ವರರೆಮಗೀ ಕೃತಿಯೊಳಕೃತಕಾಚಾರಪರರ್ (೪)
In whose mouth will compositions that feature
the greatest ornaments become famous?
May those paramount lords among poets,
devoted to their natural practice,
be of assistance to me in this work.
ಶ್ರುತದೊಳ್ ಭಾವಿಸಿ ನೋಳ್ಪೊಡೆ
ಸತತಂ ಕವಿವೃಷಭರಾ ಪ್ರಯೋಗಂಗಳೊಳಂ ಕೃತಪರಿಚಯಬಲನಪ್ಪನ
ನತಿಶಯಧವಳನ ಸಭಾಸದರ್ ಮನ್ನಿಸುವರ್ (೫)
One who acquires a strong familiarity
with the traditional knowledge and with
the usage of the bulls among poets,
meditating upon them and considering them
constantly, will find esteem among
the attendants of the court of
the one who is miraculously white.
ವ್ಯಾಕರಣ ಕಾವ್ಯನಾಟಕ
ಲೋಕಕಳಾ ಸಮಯಮಾದಳಂಕೃತಿಗಳೊಳಂ ವ್ಯಾಕುಳನಲ್ಲದನೇಕೆ ವಿ
ವೇಕಬೃಹಸ್ಪತಿಯ ನಗರಮಂ ಪುಗುತರ್ಪಂ (೬)
If one is not fully occupied with the ornaments—
grammar, poetry, drama, the worldly arts, and
conventions—then why should one enter into
the city of the Brihaspati of Judgement?
ಮೃಗಪಶುಶಕುನಿ ಗಣಂಗಳೊ
ಳಗಣಿತ ನಿಜಜಾತಿಜನಿತ ಭಾಷೆಗಳೆಂದುಂ ನೆಗೞ್ದಂತಿರೆ ನರರೊಳಮ
ಪ್ರಗಲ್ಭವಚನಪ್ರವೃತ್ತಿ ನೆಗೞ್ಗುಂ ಸಹಜಂ (೭)
Just as countless languages appear innately
in flocks of animals—wild beasts, domestic
creatures, and birds—so also in men
a faculty of speech, though not fully developed,
appears naturally.
ಗುಣಮಿದು ದೋಷಮಿದೆಂಬೀ
ಗಣಿದಮನೆತ್ತಱಿಗುಮಶ್ರುತ ಪ್ರಕೃತಿಜನಂ ತೃಣಸಸ್ಸಘಾಸವಿಷಯ
ಪ್ರಣಯಂ ಸಮವೃತ್ತಿಯಪ್ಪವೋಲ್ ಮೃಗಗಣದೊಳ್
Where will a man in his natural state,
without education, know how to reckon
what is a virtue and what is a fault?
It is as if he were among the animals,
enjoying straw, grain, or pasture,
without any preference for any of them.
ಅದಱಿಂ ಪರಮಾಗಮ ಕೋ
ವಿದನಪ್ಪುದು ಪೂರ್ವಕಾವ್ಯರಚನೆಗಳಂ ತಾಂ ಮೊದಲೊಳ್ ಕಲ್ತಂಗಲ್ಲದೆ
ಪದದೊಳ್ ಜಾಣುಂ ಬೆಡಂಗುಮಕ್ಕುಮೆ ಕೃತಿಯೊಳ್ (೯)
Therefore, apart from becoming skilled in
the highest traditions, and having first
studied the poetic compositions of the past,
will words have skill, and will
a work have beauty?
ಜನಜನಮುಮೆಲ್ಲಮೋದಂ
ತಡೆಯದೆ ಕಲ್ಗುಂ ಗುರೂಪದೇಶಕ್ರಮದಿಂ ನುಡಿವಲ್ಮೆಯಲ್ತದೇಂ ಕ
ಲ್ತೊಡನೋದುವುವಲ್ತೆ ಗಿಳಿಗಳುಂ ಪುರುಳಿಗಳುಂ (೧೦)
Nobody is learned to begin with,
but with the instruction of a teacher,
everyone can learn without difficulty.
Isn’t that the essence of language? Isn’t
it true that even parrots can teach their
young to speak after a little time?
ಪ್ರತಿಭಾವತ್ವಮುಮಕೃತಕ
ಚತುರತೆಯುಂ ಪರಮಬುಧಜನೋಪಾಸನಮುಂ ಶ್ರುತಪರಿಚಯಮುಂ ತರ್ಕುಂ
ಪ್ರತೀತಿಯಂ ವಾಗ್ವಿದಗ್ಧತಾ ನಿಪುಣತೆಯೊಳ್ (೧೧)
Insight, natural skill, association
with the greatest scholars, and the study
of texts are what bring about a reputation
for being clever and adroit in language.
ಪಸರಿಸಿದಾ ಬಗೆ ಮನಮಂ
ಪೊಸತಾಗೆ ನೆಗೞ್ಚಲರಿಯನಾ ರಚನೆಯೊಳೇಂ ಬಸನಂ ತನಗಿನಿತಱ ಬೇ
ವಸಮೇಂ ಕವರ್ದವರುಮೊಳರೆ ಕವಿಯಲ್ಲದರಂ (೧೨)
If someone cannot compose when a novel thought
occurs to his mind, why should he devote all
of his time to writing it up? What pains he takes
for such paltry results! If those people try
to force it, that doesn’t make them poets, does it?
ಬಗೆಬಗೆದು ಕೇಳ್ದು ಬಧರೊಲ
ವೊಗೆದಿರೆ ಹೃದಯದೊಳೆ ತಾಳ್ದ ಮಣಿಹಾರಂಬೋಲ್ ಸೊಗಯಿಸುವ ವಚನವಿರಚನೆ
ನೆಗೞ್ಗುಂ ಭಾವಿಸುವೊಡದಱ ಪೆಂಪತಿಸುಲಭಂ (೧೩)
A truly beautiful composition borne in your heart
will shine like a jewel necklace worn on your chest:
the more you think about it and hear it, the more
your affection for wise poets grows. Its greatness
is within easy reach, if only you meditate on it.4
ನೆನೆನೆನೆದು ಪೆಱರ ಮಾತುಗ
ಳನೆ ನೆಗೞ್ದಿರೆ ಕೃತಿಯೊಳಿಡುವವಂ ನಗದ ಗುಹಾ ಧ್ವನಿವೊಲನರ್ಥವಚನಂ
ತಲಗಾಗಿಸಲಱಿಯನುಚಿತವಾಕ್ಚತುರತೆಯಂ (೧೪)
If after thinking about the words of others again and again
someone obviously puts them into his own work,
his speech will be as meaningless as echoes in a mountain cave.
He does not know how to cultivate
the skills of language in the proper way.
ಕುಱಿತಂತು ಪೆಱರ ಬಗೆಯಂ
ತೆಱೆದಿರೆ ಪಱರ್ಗಱಿಪಲಾರ್ಪವಂ ಮಾತಱಿವಂ ಕಿಱಿದಱೊಳೆ ಪಿರಿದುವರ್ಥಮ
ನಱಿಪಲ್ ನೆಱೆವಾತನಾತನಿಂದಂ ನಿಪುಣಂ (೧೫)
More clever than him is the one who knows words,
who can open up the ideas of others by conveying
it to new audiences, who can convey a meaning,
however great, in small compass.
ನುಡಿಯಂ ಛಂದದೊಳೊಂದಿರೆ
ತೊಡರ್ಚಲಱಿವಾತನಾತನಿಂದಂ ಜಾಣಂ ತಡೆಯದೆ ಮಹಾಧ್ವಕೃತಿಗಳ
ನೊಡರಿಸಲಾರ್ಪಾತನೆಲ್ಲರಿಂದಂ ಬಲ್ಲಂ (೧೬)
More learned than him his the person who knows
how to give his speech a unity, arranging it in verse.
The wisest of all is the person who can produce
works of the Great Path in no time at all.
ಮಾತಱಿವರ್ ಕೆಲಬರ್ ಜಗ
ತೀತಳಗತ ಮನುಜರೊಳಗೆ ಮಾತಱಿವರೊಳ್ ನೀತಿವಿದರಮಳಕವಿತಾ
ನೀತಿಯುತರ್ ಕೆಲರೆ ಪರಮಕವಿವೃಷಭರ್ಕಳ್ (೧೭)
Among the men spread over the surface of
the earth, those who truly know words are few.
Among those who know words, few indeed are
those who know the principles, who possess
the principles of blameless poetry, who are
the supreme bulls among poets.
ಪಾಪಮಿದು ಪುಣ್ಯಮಿದು ಹಿತ
ರೂಪಮಿದಹಿತಪ್ರಕಾರಮಿದು ಸುಖಮಿದು ದುಃ ಖೋಪಾತ್ತಮಿದೆಂದಱಿಪುಗು
ಮಾ ಪರಮ ಕವಿಪ್ರಧಾನರಾ ಕಾವ್ಯಂಗಳ್ (೧೮)
The poems of those who are foremost
among supreme poets communicate what is bad,
what is good, what is beneficial, what is
not beneficial, what is pleasant and what is
painful to experience.
ಲೌಕಿಕ ಸಾಮಯಿಕೋರು ವಿ
ವೇಕಮುಮಭ್ಯುದಯ ಪರಮ ನಿಃಶ್ರೇಯಸಮುಂ ಪ್ರಾಕಣಮಕ್ಕುಂ ವಿದಿತ
ನೇಕ ಕವೀಶ ಪ್ರಯೋಗ ಪದಪದ್ಧತಿಯೊಳ್ (೧೯)
A single kind of awareness, both worldly and spiritual,
and the single ultimate good of success, is what
becomes clear on the footpath of the productions
of the many famous lords among poets.
ಅಧಿಕೃತ ಸತ್ಪುರುಷಾರ್ಥ
ಪ್ರಧಾನ ಧರ್ಮಾರ್ಥಕಾಮಮೋಕ್ಷಂಗಳವಾ ಬುಧಜನ ವಿವಿಕ್ತ ಕಾವ್ಯ
ಪ್ರಧಾರಿತಾರ್ಥಂಗಳಖಿಳ ಭುವನಹಿತಂಗಳ್ (೨೦)
The principal aims of good and responsible people
are duty, power, pleasure, and liberation, and it is on
these aims, which are universally beneficial, that wise poets have focused their faultless poems.
ಲೋಕದೊಳದಱಿಂ ಕಾವ್ಯ
ಶ್ರೀಕರಣೋದ್ಯುಕ್ತನಕ್ಕೆ ಕವಿ ತನ್ನಱಿವಂ ತಾಕಲ್ಪಂತಸ್ಥಾಯಿ
ಶ್ರೀಕೀರ್ತಿವಧೂಪ್ರಧಾನವಲ್ಲಭನಕ್ಕುಂ (೨೧)
That is why a poet in the world should strive to produce
beauty in his poems. The one who knows this will become
the chosen lover of two women: Fame and Fortune
lasting until the end of the age.
ಕವಿಭಾವಕೃತಾನೇಕ
ಪ್ರವಿಭಾಗ ವಿವಿಕ್ತ ಸೂಕ್ತಮಾರ್ಗಂ ಕಾವ್ಯಂ ಸವಿಶೇಷ ಶಬ್ದರಚನಂ
ವಿವಿಧಾರ್ಥವ್ಯಕ್ತಿವರ್ತಿತಾಲಂಕಾರಂ (೨೨)
Poetry is a very special kind of verbal composition.
The poet’s ideas form the many parts into which its
eloquent path is divided, and it contains figures
to express all manners of meanings.
ನರಲೋಕಚಂದ್ರಮತದಿಂ
ಪರಮಾಸಂಕಾರಮುಂ ಶರೀರಮುಮೆಂದಿಂ ತೆರಡಕ್ಕುಂ ಭೇದಂ ಬಹು
ಪರಿಕರಮಾ ಕಾವ್ಯವಸ್ತು ಪುರುಷಂಗೆದುಂ (೨೩)
According to the view of the moon in the world of men,
there is always a twofold division, with
many subordinate divisions, that applies to that
person who constitutes the substance of poetry: namely,
the highest ornament, and the body.5
ಅವಱೊಳ್ ಶರೀರಮೆಬುದು
ಕವಿಪ್ರಧಾನ ಪ್ರಯೋಗಪದಪದ್ಧತಿಯೊಳ್ ದ್ವಿವಿಧಮೆನಿಕ್ಕಮದತಿಶಯ
ಧವಳೋಕ್ತಕ್ರಮದೆ ಗದ್ಯಪದ್ಯಖ್ಯಾತಂ (೨೪)
Among them, what is called the body
on the footpath of the productions of the foremost poets
is said to be twofold. In the classification
of the one who is miraculously white, that is
known as prose and verse.
ಪದಪಾದವಿಯುತನಿಯಮಾ
ಸ್ಪದಮಲ್ಲದಳಂಕೃತಂ ಕ್ರಿಯಾಕಾರಕದೊಳ್ ಪುದಿದರ್ಥವ್ಯಕ್ತಿಯನೀ
ವುದು ಗದ್ಯಮನೇಕರೂಪಭೇದವಿವಿಕ್ತಂ (೨೫)
Ornamented, but not subject to the restriction
of putting words into metrical quarters, prose,
which is divided into many varieties,
yields the expression of a meaning that is constructed
around a verbal form and its arguments.
ಸದಮಳಸಮಸಂಸ್ಕೃತಕಾ
ವ್ಯದ ಹೃದಯಂ ಹರ್ಷಚರಿತಕಾದಂಬರಿಗಳ್ ಮೊದಲಾಗಿ ನೆಗೞ್ದವಿದಱೊಳ್
ಸದಳಂಕಾರಂಗಳಖಿಳಕವಿವಿದಿತಂಗಳ್ (೨೬)
It is the heart of poetry that is good, blameless,
and exclusively in Sanskrit, such as the Harṣacarita
and the Kādambarī. The good figures that
are conspicuous within them are well-known to all poets.6
ಮಿಗೆ ಕನ್ನಡಗಬ್ಬಂಗಳೊ
ಳಗಣಿತಗುಣಗದ್ಯಪದ್ಯಸಮ್ಮಿಶ್ರಿತಮಂ ವಿಗದಿಸುವರ್ ಗದ್ಯಕಥಾ
ಪ್ರಗೀತಿಯಿಂ ತಚ್ಚಿರಂತನಾಚಾರ್ಯರ್ಕಳ್ (೨೭)
Furthermore, the teachers of yore include
a mixture of prose and verse of countless good qualities
within Kannada works under the name of
the prose story.
ಸಕಳಕಳಾಭಾಷಾಸೌ
ಕಿಕಸಾಮಯಿಕಾದಿವರ್ಣನಾನಿರ್ಣಿಕ್ತ ಪ್ರಕಟತರವಸ್ತುವಿಸ್ತರ
ವಿಕಲ್ಪವಿದವಲ್ಲದಾಗದಾಗದು ಪೇೞಲ್ (೨೮)
If someone does not know all of the
arts and languages, how to describe
worldly and spiritual situations, and the
possibilities for developing the plot
cleanly and very clearly,
it is completely impossible to compose.
ವಿಮಳೋದಯ ನಾಗಾರ್ಜುನ
ಸಮೇತ ಜಯಬಂಧು ದುರ್ವಿನೀತಾದಿಗಳೀ ಕ್ರಮದೊಳ್ ನೆಗೞ್ಚಿ ಗದ್ಯಾ
ಶ್ರಮಪದಗುರುತಾ ಪ್ರತೀತಿಯಂ ಕೆಯ್ಕೊಂಡರ್ (೨೯)
Together with Vimalodaya and Nagarjuna,
Jayabandhu, Durvinita and so on wrote
in this manner and acquired the fame of being
gurus in the hermitage of prose.7
ಪದ್ಯಂ ಸಮಸ್ತಜನತಾ
ಹೃದ್ಯಂ ಪದವಿದಿತಪಾದನಿಯಮನಿವೇದ್ಯಂ ವಿದ್ಯಾಪಾರಪರಾಯಣ
ಮಾದ್ಯಂ ಸದ್ವೃತ್ತಿವೃತ್ತಜಾತ್ಯಾಯತ್ತಂ (೩೦)
Verse is cherished by everyone. It is characterized
by the restriction of putting words into well-known metrical quarters.
Being foremost, it ultimately leads to the furthest shore of
knowledge. It depends on the wise employment of
syllable-counting and mora-counting meters.8
ಪ್ರಣುತಗುಣಸೂರಿನಾರಾ
ಯಣಭಾರವಿಕಾಳಿದಾಸಮಾಘಾದಿಗಳೀ ಗಣಿದದೊಳೆ ಮಹಾಕಾವ್ಯ
ಪ್ರಣಯಮನಾಗಿಸಿದರಮಳಕವಿವೃಷಭರ್ಕಳ್ (೨೧)
Under this heading the spotless bulls among poets—
the celebrated Gunasuri, Narayana, Bharavi, Kalidasa
and Magha—have made us all love the Great Poem.9
ಪರಮ ಶ್ರೀವಿಜಯ ಕವೀ
ಶ್ವರ ಪಂಡಿತ ಚಂದ್ರ ಸೋಕಪಾಲಾದಿಗಳಾ ನಿರತಿಶಯ ವಸ್ತು ವಿಸ್ತರ
ವಿರಚನೆ ಲಕ್ಷ್ಯಂ ತದಾದ್ಯಕಾವ್ಯಕ್ಕೆಂದುಂ (೩೨)
The compositions of the supreme Shrivijaya, Kavishvara
Pandita, Chandra, Lokapala, and so on that have
an unsurpassable plot development have always been
the goal for the poetry that comes after them.10
ನುಡಿಗೆಲ್ಲಂ ಸಲ್ಲದ ಕ
ನ್ನಡದೊಳ್ ಚತ್ತಾಣಮುಂ ಬೆದಂಡೆಯುಮೆಂದೀ ಗಡಿನ ನೆಗೞ್ತೆಯ ಕಬ್ಬದೊ
ಳೊಡಂಬಡಂ ಮಾಡಿದರ್ ಪುರಾತನ ಕವಿಗಳ್ (೩೩)
The poets of old formed a consensus on commemorative
poetry which belonged to the categories of
cattāṇaṃ and bedaṇḍe
in Kannada, which are not suitable for every language.
ಕಂದಮುಮಮಳಿನ ವೃತ್ತಮು
ಮೊಂದೊಂದೆಡೆಗೊಂಡು ಜಾತಿಜಾಣೆಸೆಯೆ ಬೆಡಂ ಗೊದಿವಱೊಳಮರೆ ಪೇೞಲ್
ಸುಂದರ ರೂಪ ಬೆದಂಡೆಗಬ್ಬಮದಕ್ಕುಂ (೩೪)
When the kanda verse-form and the spotless
syllable-counting meters each occur in their place, and knowledge
of the mora-counting meters shines forth, and a special
kind of beauty appears in all of them, that composition is
the bedaṇḍe poem, beautiful in form.11
ಕಂದಂಗಳ್ ಫಲವಾಗಿರೆ
ಸುಂದರ ವೃತ್ತಂಗಳಕ್ಕರಂ ಚವಪದಿ ಮ ತ್ತಂ ದಲ್ ಗೀತಿಕೆ ತಿವದಿಗ
ಳಂದಂಬೆತ್ತೆಸೆಯೆ ಪೇೞ್ವೊಡದು ಚತ್ತಾಣಂ (೩೫)
If the bulbs, which are the kanda verse-forms,
develop into fruits, which are the beautiful verse-forms called
akkaraṃ, cavupadi, and of course gītike
and tivadi, and they appear in a beautiful manner,
that kind of composition is called cattāṇaṃ.12
ಕಾವೇರಿಯಿಂದಮಾ ಗೋ
ದಾವರಿವರಮಿರ್ಪ ನಾಡದಾ ಕನ್ನಡದೊಳ್ ಭಾವಿಸಿದ ಜನಪದಂ ವಸು
ಧಾ ವಳಯ ವಿಲೀನ ವಿಶದ ವಿಷಯ ವಿಶೇಷಂ (೩೬)
The land which extends from the Kaveri to the Godavari River
is the country that we have in mind in Kannada,
a very special and splendid region within the circle of the earth.
ಆದಱೊಳಗಂ ಕಿಸುವೊೞಲಾ
ವಿದಿತ ಮಹಾಕೊಪಣ ನಗರದಾ ಪುಲಿಗೆಱಿಯಾ ತದಭಿಸ್ತುತಮಪ್ಪೊಂಕುಂ
ದದ ವಡುವನ ನಾಡೆ ಕನ್ನಡದ ತಿರುಳ್ (೩೭)
Within it, the country that lies in the middle
of Kisuvol̤al, the famous city of Mahākopaṇam,
Puliger̤i, and the highly praised Oṅkundam,
is the essence of Kannada.13
ಪದನಱಿದು ನುಡಿಯಲುಂ ನುಡಿ
ದುದನಱಿದಾರಯಲುಮಾರ್ಪರಾ ನಾಡವರ್ಗಳ್ ಚದುರರ್ ನಿಜದಿಂ ಕುಱಿತೋ
ದದೆಯುಂ ಕಾವ್ಯ ಪ್ರಯೋಗ ಪರಿಣತ ಮತಿಗಳ್ (೩೮)
The people of that land can speak with a knowledge
of what is proper and can carefully reflect with a knowledge of what has been spoken.
They are clever by nature. Despite never having been
taught, their minds are transformed by the practice of poetry.
ಕುಱಿತವರಲ್ಲದೆ ಮತ್ತಂ
ಪೆಱರುಂ ತಂತಮ್ಮ ನುಡಿಯೊಳೆಲ್ಲರ್ ಜಾಣರ್ ಕಿಱುವಕ್ಕಳುಮಾ ಮಾಗರು
ಮಱಿ ಪಲ್ಕಱಿವರ್ ವಿವೇಕಮಂ ಮಾತುಗಳಂ (೩೯)
But it is not just them. Others, too, are knowledgable
when it comes to their own speech: small children,
the mute—they know the words to convey their understanding.14
ಜಾಣರ್ಕಳಲ್ಲದವರುಂ
ಪೂಣಿಗರಱಿಯದೆಯುಮಱಿವವೋಲವಗುಣದಾ ತಾಣಮನಿನಿಸೆಡೆವೆತ್ತೊಡೆ
ಮಾಣದೆ ಪಿಡಿದದನೆ ಕೃತಿಗಳಂ ಕೆಡೆ ನುಡಿವರ್ (೪೦)
Even people who are not learned are fastidious;
they act as if they know when they don’t. If the smallest
opportunity for criticism becomes available, they
take it without hesitation and denounce works.15
ಸಕ್ಕದಮುಂ ಪಾಗದಮುಮ
ದಕ್ಕುಂ ಬಗೆದಂತೆ ಸಮಱಿ ಪೇೞಲ್ ಮುನ್ನಂ ನಿಕ್ಕುವಮೊಳವಪ್ಪದಱಿಂ
ತಕ್ಕಂತವಱವಱಿ ಲಕ್ಷ್ಯೃಮುಂ ಲಕ್ಷಣಮುಂ (೪೧)
Sanskrit and Prakrit have long been available
for composing fine works as one sees fit, true enough.
For that reason, there are plenty of works that set out
the rules and exemplify them for both of them.
ಅರಿದಾದಂ ಕನ್ನಡದ
ಳ್ ತಿರಿಕೊಱೆಗೊಂಡಱಿಯೆ ಪೇೞ್ವೆನೆಂಬುದಿದಾರ್ಗಂ ಪರಮಾಚಾರ್ಯರವೋಲ್ ಸೈ
ತಿರಲಱಿಯರ್ ಕನ್ನಡಕ್ಕೆ ನಾಡವರೋಜರ್ (೪೨)1
It is basically impossible for anyone who knows
what is like to whirl around helplessly in Kannada
to claim that he can compose. Like famous professors
they don’t know how to keep quiet: when it comes to Kannada,
the teachers are the natives.
ಅವಗುಣಮಿನಿತಾದೊಡಮಾ
ಕವಿತಾಬಂಧಮನಶೇಷಮಂ ದೂಷಿಸಗುಂ ಸವಿಳಾಸಲೋಲಲೋಚನ
ವಿವರಮನೆಡೆವೆತ್ತ ಕಸದವೋಲನವರತಂ (೪೩)
If there is the smallest point of weakness, it
will ruin the entire poetic composition
always, like filth that appears in the orifice
of woman with beautiful rolling eyes.
ಅದಱಿಂ ದಲಸದೆ ಪೀನಂ
ಪದೆಯದೆ ಪಾಂಗಱಿದು ದೋಷಮಂ ಪಿಂಗಿಸಿ ತ ಪ್ಪದೆ ಬಗೆದು ಪೇೞ್ವುದಾಗಮ
ಮುದಾರ ನೃಪತುಂಗ ದೇವ ವಿದಿತ ಕ್ರಮದಿಂ (೪೪)
That is why this teaching is being set forth, tirelessly
and extensively, with technique but without faults,
without fail and thoughtfully, according to the
well-known system of the exalted Nripatunga.
ಕಾಣನೇಗೆಯ್ದುಂ ತನ್ನ ದೋಷಮಂ
ಕಾಣದಂತೆಂದುಂ ಕಣ್ಗಳ್ ತಮ್ಮ ಕಾಡಿಗೆಯಂ ಪೂಣಿಗನಾದುದಱಿಂ ಪೆಱರಿಂ
ಜಾಣರಿನೋದಿಸಿ ಪೇೞ್ವುದು ಕಬ್ಬಮಂ (೪೫)
Whatever his mistakes may be, he doesn’t see them,
like the eyes can never see the collyrium applied to them.
That is why the fastidious poet will have other learned people
read through the poetry that he composes.16
ದೋಷಮಿನಿತೆಂದು ಬಗೆದು
ದ್ಭಾಸಿಸಿ ತಱಿಸಂದು ಕನ್ನಡಂಗಳೊಳೆಂದುಂ ವಾಸುಗಿಯುಮಱಿಯಲಾಱದೆ
ಬೇಸಱುಗುಂ ದೇಶೇ ಬೇಱೆವೇಱಪ್ಪುದಱಿಂ (೪೬)
If he had to consider how great or small the fault is,
throw some light on it, and make a judgement
all the time for all Kannada usages,
even the thousand-headed Vasuki would be at a loss
and become weary, given how varied the Regional is.
ಅಂತುಂ ಪುರಾತಣಕವಿಗಳ
ಸಂತ(ತ)ಗತ ಮಾರ್ಗಗದಿತ ದೋಷಂಗಳುಮಂ ಚಿಂತಿಸಿ ಮತ್ತೆನ್ನಱಿವುದು
ಮಂ ತಱಿಸ(ಲೆ) ಬುಧಜನಕ್ಕೆ ಕೆಲವಮ ಪೇೞ್ವೆಂ (೪೭)
And thus, after considering even the faults
in the Ways ceaselessly traversed by poets of old,
I will speak of the little that I know
for the learned to consider.
ನೆಲಸಿದ ಕಾವ್ಯಂ ಕಾವ್ಯ
ಕ್ಕೆ ತಕ್ಷಣಂ ಸತತಮೆದೆ ಪೞಗನ್ನಡಮಂ ಪೊಲಗೆಡಿಸಿ ನುಡಿವರಾಗಮ
ಬಲಹೀನರ್ ದೇಸಿಯಲ್ಲದೆಂದಱಿರ್ದುಂ (೪೮)
Those who are weak in the traditional teaching, and
claim that the rules of poetry should always be based
on established poetry, ruin Old Kannada when they speak it
even when they are aware that it is not the Regional.
ಪೊಡವಿಪತಿ ಗೊರವನೆನೆ ಪೇ
ೞ್ಪಡೆಯದುವದೆಕರಿಸಿ ಕಬ್ಬಿಗರ್ದೂಱೆನೆಯುಂ ಹಡುವಸುಮಱಸೆಟ್ಟಿಸು ಗೆಂ
ಮುಡಿಯೊಳಮಾರ್ಗೆಂದು ನುಡಿವ ಪೞಗನ್ನಡಮಂ (೪೯)
poḍavipati goravan ene pē
l̤paḍeyad uvade karisi kabbigar dūr̤eneyuṃ
haḍuvasu mar̤as- eṭṭisu geṃ-
muḍiyoḷam is what Old Kannada sounds like.17
ದೊರೆಕೊಂಡಿರೆ ಸೊಗಯಿಸುಗುಂ
ಪುರಾಣ ಕಾವ್ಯ ಪ್ರಯೋಗದೊಳ್ ತತ್ಕಾಲಂ ವಿರಸಂ ಕರಮವು ದೇಸಿಗೆ
ಜರದ್ವಧೂವಿಷಯ ಸುರತ ರಸರಸಿಕತೆವೋಲ್ (೫೦)
When they appear in the usage of old poems,
they are beautiful. When it comes to the Regional
at the present moment, they are absolutely revolting,
as if someone had a special taste for making love to old women.
ಸಮಸಂಸ್ಕೃತಂಗಳೊಳ್ ಸ
ಯ್ತಮರ್ದಿರೆ ಕನ್ನಡಮನಱೆದು ಪೇೞ್ಗೆಂಬುದಿದಾ ಗಮಕೋವಿದ ನಿಗದಿತ ಮಾ
ರ್ಗಮಿದಂ ಬೆರಸಲ್ಕಮಾರದೀ ಸಕ್ಕದದೊಳ್ (೫೧)
If someone knows Kannada, let him write it
in a way that neatly blends with Sanskrit-identical words.
This is the Way prescribed by those who are skilled
in the traditions. It is not proper to mix it with
the Sanskrit words that follow.
ಅಹರಹರುಚ್ಚೈರ್ನೀಚೈ
ರ್ಮುಹುರ್ಮುಹುರಿತಸ್ತತಃ ಪುನಃಪುನರಂತ ರ್ಬಹಿರಾದಿಹ ಪ್ರಾದುರಹೋ
ಸಹಸಾದಿಗಳವ್ಯಯಂಗಳಸಹಾಯಂಗಳ್ (೫೨)
Indeclinables such as aharahaḥ “day by day,”
uccaiḥ “loud,” nīcaiḥ “soft,”
muhurmuhuḥ “moment by moment,”
itastataḥ “here and there,”
punaḥpunaḥ “again and again,”
antaḥ “inside,” bahiḥ “outside,”
the adposition ā, iha “here,”
prāduḥ “evident,” ahō “oh!” and
gsahasā “immediately,” when they are used alone.18
ಬೆರಸಿರೆ ಕನ್ನಡದೊಳ್ ಬಂ
ಧುರಮಾಗದು ಕಾವ್ಯರಚನೆ ಪೇೞ್ದೊಡೆ ಪೀನಂ ಪರುಷತರಮಕ್ಕುಮೊತ್ತುಂ
ಗರಡೆಯ ಮದ್ದಳಿಯ ಜರ್ಝರ ಧ್ವನಿಗಳವೋಲ್ (೫೩)
When these are mixed into Kannada it will never
be charming. If you compose poetry like this, it will be
extremely harsh, like the hollow sounds of the
maddaḷe and the tight-stretched karaḍe drums.19
ಬಹಿರಂತರುಪವನಂಗಳೊ
ಳಹರಹಮಾ ಪರಭೃತಂಗಳುಲಿವ ರವಂಗಳ್ ಸಹಸಾ ತಳಮಳಗೊಳಿಸಿದ
ವಹೋ ಮನಂಗಳನಿತಸ್ತತಂ ವಿರಹಿಗಳಾ (೫೪)
In the groves, outside and within, day after day,
the sounds made by the cuckoos all around
instantly torment, alas, the hearts
of separated lovers here and there.
ವಿದಿತಸಮಸಂಸ್ಕೃತೋದಿತ
ಪದಂಗಳೊಳ್ ಪುದಿದು ಬೆರಸಿ ಬರೆ ಕನ್ನಡದೊಳ್ ಮುದಮನವು ತರ್ಕುಮತಿಶಯ
ಮೃದಂಗ ಸಂಗೀತಕಾದಿ ಮಧುರರವಂಬೋಲ್
If they appear in Kannada in close combination
with words that are clearly Sanskrit-identical
they will bring pleasure, like the sweet sounds of
musical pieces with the excellent tabour drum.
ಬಹಿರುದ್ಯಾನಾಂತರದೊಳ್
ಸಹಸೋದಿತಮತ್ತಕೋಕಿಲೋಚ್ಚೈರ್ಧ್ವಾನಂ ಮುಹುರಾಕರ್ಣಿಸೆ ಪಡೆದ
ತ್ತಹರ್ನಿಶಂ ಪೆರ್ಚನಂತರಗಕ್ಕೆನ್ನಾ

ದ್ವಿತೀಯ ಜರಿಚ್ಛೇದಂ

Second Chapter

ತೃತೀಯ ಜರಿಚ್ಛೇದಂ

Third Chapter

Notes to the text

1I take ತಿರಿಕೊಱೆಗೊಂಡು as a verbal formation from the bases ತಿರಿ “turn around” and ಕೊಱೆ “whirl about.”

Notes to the translation

1Verse 1 compares the patron and central figure of the text, the Rashtrakuta King Nripatunga Amoghavarsha, to the god Vishnu: Vishnu is the husband of Shri (or Lakshmi), and the luminous Kaustubha jewel resides on his chest.

2These are all epithets of Nripatunga Amoghavarsha.

3Sarasvati, the goddess of learning and literature, is compared to a Royal Goose (rājahaṃsī), a bird that is said to live at Lake Manasa in the Himalayas.

4The comparison between a beautiful poem and a beautiful jewel necklace seems to have two aspects: one is that they are both, in a sense, carried hṛdayadoḷe (in the heart, in the case of the poem, and on the chest, in the case of the necklace); the other is that a necklace often serves in Indian literature as a token of love, and thus reminds the wearer of the person who presented it.

5The moon in the world of men is another epithet of Nripatunga Amoghavarsha.

6The Harṣacarita and the Kādambarī are two famous prose works in Sanskrit by Bāṇa.

7Durvinita was a king of the Ganga dynasty in the sixth century. None of his works survive. The other authors are unknown.

8Syllable-counting and mora-counting meters (vṛtta and jāti are the principal subdivisions of metrical forms in Indian metrics.

9Great Poem (mahākāvya), the principal genre of verse composition in Sanskrit.

10Most of the poets named in this verse, with the exception of Shrivijaya of course, are unknown.

11From this description the bedaṇḍe appears to have been a polymetric composition that used the syllable- and mora-counting meters (vṛttas and jātis) which were also used in Sanskrit and Prakrit poetry.

12From this description the cattāṇaṃ also appears to have been a polymetric composition, but using a variety of meters that are only found in Kannada (which are now called aṃśa-counting meters).

13/?/ Identify these places.

14they know the words to convey their understanding: the syntax is difficult; alternatively “they know how to convey their understanding and their words.”

15The word translated as “fastidious” here, pūṇiga, occurs several times and its meaning is unclear. I translate based on the context. It seems to be related to the stem pūṇ “swear,” and in later texts it has the meaning of “hero.”

16Lamp-black was used to treat ocular conditions by direct application to the eye; although the dark coloration of the patient’s eye is visible to others, his own vision is not changed.

17This verse, which is meant to exemplify Old Kannada, contains a number of words and expressions that are presently unintelligible.

18These are all indeclinables, functioning as adverbs. They are prohibited from being used on their own (or, in the case of antarbahir and so on, with other indeclinables), that is, without entering into a compound with another Sanskrit word. The word ā has been cited in the form āt, with the letter t being used, as often in Sanskrit grammar, to refer to a vowel sound of a specified length in contexts where sandhi would make either the quality or the length of the vowel unclear.

19The maddaḷe (mardala in Sanskrit) and the karaḍe (karaṭa in Sanskrit) are two different types of drum.
