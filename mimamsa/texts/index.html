    Texts and Authors

    (in roughly chronological order)

    Jaimini

    Date: Unknown (often put to 2nd or 1st c. BCE)
    Works:
    • Mīmāṁsāsūtras: The foundational work of the Mīmāṁsā system (Pandit).

    The Vr̥ttikāra

    Often identified with Bhavadāsa, a pre-Śabara commentator on the Mīmāṁsāsūtra. He is called the Vr̥ttikāra by Śabara, who quotes him extensively at 1.1.5.

    Śabara

    Date: 5th century CE
    Works:
    • Mīmāṁsāsūtrabhāṣya or Śābarabhāṣya: A commentary on the sūtras of Jaimini. Large portions of an earlier commentary, ascribed to one “Vr̥ttikāra,” are included at 1.1.5. (Pandit)
    Refers to: Vr̥ttikāra

    Kumārila

    Date: 6th century
    Works:
    • Mīmāṁsāślōkavārttika: A commentary on the first pāda of Śabara’s Bhāṣya (the so-called tarkapāda) (Pandit).
    • Tantravārttika: A commentary on Śabara’s Bhāṣya, running from 1.2 to 3.8 (Pandit).
      • Ānandāśrama Sanskrit Series No. 97:
        • Part 2.1 (1929): [source?]
        • Part 2.2 (1930): Internet Archive
        • Part 3 (1931): Internet Archive
    • Ṭupṭīkā: A commentary on the rest of the Mīmāṁsā system, from 4.1 to 12.4 (Pandit).
      • Benares 1904 (ed. Gangâdhara Śâstrî). Internet Archive.
    • Br̥haṭṭīkā (lost) (Pandit)
    Quotations

    Kumārila quotes Bhartr̥hari (both the Vākyapadīya and the Dīpikā in the Vyākaraṇādhikaraṇa, where he refers to him as Ṭīkākāra.

    Prabhākara

    Date: 7th century
    Works:
    • Br̥hatī (also known as Br̥hatī Ṭīkā (Pandit): A commentary on Śabara’s Bhāṣya (Pandit).
    • Laghvī (lost): Apparently a briefer commentary on Śabara’s Bhāṣya (Pandit)

    Maṇḍana

    Date: early 8th century
    Place: Mithilā?
    Works:
    • Mīmāṁsānukramaṇika (Pandit)
    • Brahmasiddhi (Pandit)
    • Sphōṭasiddhi (Pandit)
    • Bhāvanāvivēka (Pandit)
    • Vidhivivēka (Pandit)
    • Vibhramavivēka (Pandit)

    Umbeka

    Date: 8th century
    Place: Mithilā?
    Works:
    • Tātparyaṭīkā: A commentary on the Ślōkavārttika of Kumārila. (Pandit)
    • A commentary on Maṇḍana’s Bhāvanāvivēka (Pandit)

    Śālikanātha

    Date: 8th century
    Place: Bengal?
    Works:
    • R̥juvimalā Pañcikā: A commentary on the Br̥hatī of Prabhākara (not extant in its entirety) (Pandit)
    • Dīpaśikhā: A commentary on the Laghvī of Prabhākara, surviving only in fragments (Pandit)
    • Bhāṣyapariśiṣta: A commentary on the Bhāṣya of Śabara (Pandit)
    • Prakaraṇapañcikā: A set of essays (Pandit), including:
      • Śāstramukha
      • Nītipatha
      • Nayavīthi
      • Jātinirṇaya
      • Pramāṇapārāyaṇa (covering the six instruments of knowledge: note that the kārikās are given separately as Amr̥takalā)
      • Vimalāñjana
      • Tattvālōka
      • Nyāyaśuddhi
      • Mīmāṁsājīvarakṣa
      • Vākyārthamātr̥kā in two parts
      • Aṅgapārāyaṇa in two parts
      • Atidēśapārāyaṇa
    • A lost commentary on the Praśastapādabhāṣya of Praśastapāda (a Vaiśēṣika work)

    Sucarita

    Date: 10th century
    Place: Unknown
    Works:
    • Kāśikā: A commentary on the Ślōkavārttika of Kumārila

    Paritōṣa

    Date: 12th century?
    Works:
    • Ajitā: A commentary on the Tantravārttika of Kumārila

    Pārthasārathi

    Date: 11th century?
    Works:
    • Nyāyaratnākara: a commentary on the Ślōkavārttika of Kumārila (Pandit)
    • Nyāyaratnamālā: A set of essays (Pandit), including
      • Prayuktitilaka (pp. 1–29)
      • Svataḥprāmāṇyanirṇaya (pp. 30–39)
      • Vidhinirṇaya (pp. 40–55)
      • Vyāptivāda (pp. 56–72)
      • Vākyārthanirṇaya (pp. 73–102)
      • Nityakāmyavivēka (pp. 103–119)
      • Aṅganirṇaya in five parts (pp. 120–212)
    • Tantraratna: A commentary on the Ṭupṭīkā of Kumārila (Pandit)
    • Śāstradīpikā: A commentary on the Bhāṣya of Śabara (Pandit)

    Bhavanātha

    Date: 11th century?
    Works:
    • Nayavivēka: a commentary on the first three sūtras of the Mīmāṁsā system (Pandit)

    Bhavadēva

    Date: 12th century
    Works:
    • Tautātitamatatilaka: A commentary on the Tantravārttika of Kumārila (Pandit)

    Sōmēśvara

    Date: 13th century?
    Works:
    • Nyāyasudhā: A commentary on the Tantravārttika of Kumārila (Pandit).
