lesson 16

śnuśnamuśnāḥ

Nasal presents

Goals

prāpyāṇi

  • Understand the formation of the present stem in the nasal classes, i.e., classes 5 and 8, and 7 and 9, in the active voice, in the present and imperfect tenses. Practice them with the exercise below.

1. Verb practice

Translate the following sentences into Sanskrit. Perform sandhi between words. Use the vocabulary from this lesson.

  1. I don’t know.
    • √jñā is optionally ātmanēpadi when intransitive.
  2. Do you (pl.) know her?
  3. What are we (pl.) doing?
  4. He is making (√tan) something.
  5. One buys, another sells.
  6. They didn’t know what he did.
  7. Did you (sg.) hear?
  8. They (pl.) are picking flowers.
  9. They reached the house before night.
  10. Those two are doing something.
  11. These two are not doing anything.
  12. I heard a sound.
  13. They eat standing up.
  14. He eats sitting down.
  15. What word did you use?
  16. They are able to do it.
  17. You (pl.) are eating.
  18. Who sold it?
  19. We (du.) do whatever they say.
  20. Whoever uses this knowledge attains bliss.
2. Noun practice

Translate the following short phrases into Sanskrit.

  1. Two names (nom.)
  2. The king’s many names (nom.)
  3. The truth (nom.) of the soul
  4. The king’s dog (acc.)
  5. At the birth (loc.) of the king
  6. By the dog (instr.)
  7. By the self (instr.) and from the self (abl.)
  8. Of the kings (gen.pl.)
  9. For the young man (dat.)
  10. By the two kings (instr.)
  11. By the actions (instr.) of the young man
  12. Among the kings (loc.pl.)
  13. Of the names (gen.pl.)
  14. For the young men (dat.pl.)
  15. Of karma and the self (gen.)
  16. King! (voc.)
