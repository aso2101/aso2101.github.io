Puṣpitāgrā

Pattern

n · n · r · y · n · j · j · r · g

varṇavr̥ttam
→ ardhasamavr̥ttam

Description

The puṣpitāgrā meter is the most common of the ardhasamavr̥ttam meters. It contains 50 syllables in total: the odd quarters have twelve syllables, and the even quarters have thirteen. Its syllabic pattern is as follows:

1 and 3: । । । । । । ऽ । ऽ । ऽ ऽ

2 and 4: । । । । ऽ । । ऽ । ऽ । ऽ ऽ

Definitions

Piṅgala, Chandaḥsūtram 5.41:

puṣpitāgrā nau ryau njau jrau g

Nāṭyaśāstram 15.186:

nau ryau tu prathamē pādē njau jrau gaś ca tathāparē
yatra tat puṣpitāgrā syād yadi śēṣaṁ tu pūrvavat

Ratnamañjūṣā 2.26:

puṣpitāgraṁ hitē huntau

Jānāśrayī 3.11:

puṣpitāgrā nīḍ dāśr̥

Jayadeva, Chandaḥśāstram 3.10:

ayug iha yadi nau rayau ca yugmē
najajaragāḥ kathayanti puṣpitāgram

Jayakīrti, Chandōnuśāsanam 3.12:

nagaṇayugarayēṇa puṣpitāgrā
najajaragair viditā jinavratāṁśaiḥ

Kedārabhaṭṭa, Vr̥ttaratnākaraḥ 4.10:

ayuji nayugarēphatō yakārō
yuji canajau jaragāś ca puṣpitāgrā

Hemacandra, Chandōnuśāsanam 3.16:

gāntaṁ puṣpitāgrā

Examples

Raghuvaṁśaḥ 6.86

This example was recited by H. V. Nagaraja Rao and recorded by Gil Ben-Herut in 2006. The translation is mine.

pramuditavarapakṣam ēkatas tat kṣitipatimaṇḍam anyatō vitānam uṣasi sara iva praphullapadmaṁ kumudavanapratipannanidram āsīt

Within the group of kings, the groom’s party, on the one hand,
was delighted, while all of the others were out of sorts.
It was like a pond at dawn, when the day-lotuses start to open,
and the water lilies get to sleep.
